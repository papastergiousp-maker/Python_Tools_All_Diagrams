Analyst Commentary Synthesis · 12 official annual reports · 4 Greek systemic banks · 2022–2024
Rate Cycle Tailwind — and the Compression Ahead
The ECB's 450bp hiking cycle (Jul 2022 – Sep 2023) drove sector NII from €5.5bn to €8.6bn — a 55% surge in two years. Greek banks benefited disproportionately: a high share of variable-rate corporate loans meant repricing was near-immediate. As the ECB pivoted to cuts from June 2024, NIM compression becomes the dominant 2025 risk. Eurobank and Piraeus, with larger floating-rate loan books, face greater rate sensitivity than NBG's more fee-and-spread-based income profile.
Efficiency Leaders: Piraeus and Eurobank
Piraeus leads on cost efficiency at 31.8% C/I in 2024, improving from 34.5% in 2022 as VES-driven headcount reductions and digitalisation lowered the cost base. Eurobank follows closely at 32.6%. Both are well below the European banking average of ~55%. NBG (36.5%) and Alpha (37.7%) lag — NBG's ratio reflects a large domestic retail branch footprint, while Alpha faces structural cost pressure from its South East Europe network. As the rate-driven revenue tailwind fades into 2025, cost control separates the outperformers from the rest.
Capital Accumulation and Shareholder Returns
All four banks improved CET1 ratios by 1.5–3pp between 2022 and 2024, funded by retained earnings and NPL deleveraging. NBG leads at ~19%, providing headroom for buybacks and special dividends. Piraeus completed its NPL cleanup in 2023 (impairments −65% from 2022 peak), rebuilding capital headroom after years of heavy provisioning. Eurobank's CET1 of ~18% supports continued M&A — the Hellenic Bank (Cyprus) integration contributed to the +26.7% asset growth recorded in 2024.
Alpha Bank: The Structural Laggard
Alpha's ROE of 8.2% is the weakest among Greek peers and below the ~11% European banking average. Flat NII in 2024 (−0.3% YoY) and the sector's highest Loan-to-Deposit ratio (76.5%) limit near-term upside. M&A speculation — including reported Unicredit interest — reflects investor pressure for a structural fix. Until the cost base is addressed and the rate headwind to NII is absorbed, Alpha is likely to trade at a sustained discount to its three Greek peers on a price-to-book basis.
Investment Implications Based on FY2022–2024 fundamentals · What the data says for a buy-side or sell-side analyst
▲ Overweight
NBG
ROA 1.54% • CET1 19.1%
Sector leader on both profitability and capital strength — a rare combination. CET1 of 19.1% is 650bp above regulatory floor, creating buyback/dividend optionality. NIM at 3.14% is the highest in the peer group: best positioned if ECB rate cuts are gradual.
→ Hold
Eurobank
C/I 32.6% • CET1 17.6%
Best cost efficiency in the peer group and proven M&A execution (Hellenic Bank: +27% assets, accretive day-one). Fee income share provides partial insulation against NIM compression. Deserves a quality premium on P/B; already partially priced in.
▼ Underweight
Piraeus Bank
CET1 13.7% • CoR at 10yr low
2024 profit surge (+35%, €788m → €1,066m) driven primarily by impairment release (€404m → €181m). The ~€165m after-tax impairment benefit accounts for ~60% of the profit increase; strip it out and underlying profit growth was approximately +14% — real but modest. Credit cost normalisation toward the 2023 level would reduce net profit by €120–150m (~12–14%). Lowest CET1 in the peer group (320bp above regulatory floor) limits near-term capital return capacity.
→ Monitor
Alpha Bank
ROE 8.2% • C/I 37.7%
Structural laggard: ROE of 8.2% is the weakest in the peer group and below the ~11% European banking average. C/I improvement story is real (49% → 38%) but incomplete. If Alpha closes the gap to 33–34% C/I by 2026, earnings uplift would be ~€200m. Upside is recovery-dependent.

📊 SQL — Window Function
SELECT bank, year, nii,
  ROUND((nii - LAG(nii)
  OVER (PARTITION BY bank
  ORDER BY year)) * 100.0 /
  LAG(nii) OVER (...), 1)
  AS nii_yoy_pct
FROM kpis

💡 SQL — ROE Calculation
SELECT i.bank,
  ROUND(i.net_profit * 100.0
  / b.equity, 1) AS roe_pct
FROM income_statement i
JOIN balance_sheet b
  ON i.bank = b.bank
  AND i.year = b.year
